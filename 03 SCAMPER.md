# SCAMPER

A checklist for transforming something that already exists through seven lenses: **Substitute**, **Combine**, **Adapt**, **Modify**, **Put to other use**, **Eliminate**, **Reverse**. Works especially well for product iteration, feature ideation, and stuck projects where the blank-page approach has stalled.

**1. Pick a subject.** SCAMPER works on a concrete thing: a product, feature, process, or campaign. The more specific, the more useful.

- _"Our partner onboarding portal"_ works better than _"our partner program."_

**2. Substitute.** What can you swap out? Components, people, processes, channels.

- _"What if we replaced the live training webinar with async video modules?"_
- _"What if the customer self-onboarded through an interactive wizard instead of a call?"_

**3. Combine.** What can you merge? Two features, two audiences, two parallel workflows.

- _"What if we bundled the monitoring dashboard with the billing console into one MSP view?"_

**4. Adapt.** What can you borrow from another industry, competitor, or historical precedent?

- _"How do airlines handle last-minute cancellations? Can we adapt that for SaaS subscription downgrades?"_

**5. Modify (magnify or minify).** Make it bigger, smaller, faster, slower, more or less frequent.

- _"What if the free tier included 10x more storage?"_
- _"What if the setup wizard had one question instead of twelve?"_

**6. Put to other use.** Who else could use this? What other problems could it solve?

- _"Could our admin console be useful to end users, not just IT managers?"_

**7. Eliminate.** What can you remove entirely? Steps, features, approvals, fields.

- _"What if we killed the homepage and sent trial users straight to the product?"_

**8. Reverse (rearrange).** Flip the order, direction, or roles.

- _"What if the customer wrote the proposal and we approved it?"_
- _"What if billing happened before usage instead of after?"_

**Practical notes:** Do not skim all seven lenses. Go deep on two or three. **Eliminate** and **Reverse** are the most productive and most underused. Teams find them uncomfortable because they challenge the existing setup. Yet, that discomfort is a signal to dig in. Pairs well with classic brainstorming as structured provocation when a session stalls.
