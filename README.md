# Problem-Solving and Decision-Making Toolkit

Most teams default to the same two moves when they get stuck: an unstructured brainstorm or a long meeting that ends without a decision. Yet, there are dozens of proven techniques, each designed for a specific kind of problem, that produce better outcomes in less time.

This is a collection of 33 reference cards, one per method. Each card gives you the what, the when, and a walkthrough you can run in your next meeting. Every card is written from the perspective of someone who has actually used these in business: product planning, marketing strategy, incident response, team operations.

Pick the method that fits the problem.

## How to Use

Each card follows the same structure:

1. **Opening** – what the method is, when to reach for it
2. **Six steps** – concrete enough to facilitate directly from the card
3. **Practical notes** – trade-offs, common mistakes, what to pair it with

Cards are numbered by category. If you are unsure which method to use, start with the category that matches your situation below.

## Ideation (01–07)

When you need to generate options, not evaluate them.

| # | Method | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| 01 | [Classic Brainstorming](01%20Classic%20Brainstorming.md) | Separate idea generation from evaluation. Volume first, judgment later. |
| 02 | [Brainwriting (6-3-5)](02%20Brainwriting%20(6-3-5).md) | Six people write three ideas per round, five minutes each. Brainstorming without the loudest-voice problem. |
| 03 | [SCAMPER](03%20SCAMPER.md) | Seven transformation lenses (Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to other use, Eliminate, Reverse) applied to something that already exists. |
| 04 | [Mind Mapping](04%20Mind%20Mapping.md) | Radial, associative thinking on paper. Central topic branches outward into subtopics and connections. |
| 05 | [Crazy 8s](05%20Crazy%208s.md) | Eight sketched ideas in eight minutes. Speed kills perfectionism and surfaces unexpected solutions. |
| 06 | [Random Stimulus](06%20Random%20Stimulus.md) | Force a connection between your problem and something unrelated to break established thinking patterns. |
| 07 | [Role-Storming](07%20Role-Storming.md) | Brainstorming in character. Adopt another person's perspective to bypass your own mental blocks. |

## Problem Analysis (08–14)

When you need to understand the problem before solving it.

| # | Method | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| 08 | [Socratic Thinking](08%20Socratic%20Thinking.md) | Disciplined sequence of question types to stress-test claims, surface assumptions, and trace implications. |
| 09 | [5 Whys](09%205%20Whys.md) | Ask "why" five times in succession to drill past symptoms into root causes. |
| 10 | [Fishbone (Ishikawa)](10%20Fishbone%20(Ishikawa).md) | Map possible causes of a problem across predefined categories. Catches multiple parallel causes at once. |
| 11 | [Jobs to be Done](11%20Jobs%20to%20be%20Done.md) | Reframe product thinking by asking what the customer is actually hiring your product to accomplish. |
| 12 | [First Principles Thinking](12%20First%20Principles%20Thinking.md) | Strip a problem to fundamental truths, then rebuild reasoning from scratch. |
| 13 | [Problem Reversal](13%20Problem%20Reversal.md) | Flip the question: ask how to guarantee the opposite of your goal, then invert the answers. |
| 14 | [How Might We (HMW)](14%20How%20Might%20We%20(HMW).md) | Convert problems into generative questions that open up solution space without committing to direction. |

## Evaluation and Prioritization (15–22)

When you have options and need to decide which ones matter.

| # | Method | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| 15 | [SWOT Analysis](15%20SWOT%20Analysis.md) | Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats. Four quadrants to size up a strategic situation. |
| 16 | [Decision Matrix](16%20Decision%20Matrix%20(Weighted%20Scoring).md) | Structured comparison: list options, weight criteria, score each option. Makes reasoning transparent. |
| 17 | [Pre-Mortem](17%20Pre-Mortem.md) | Imagine the project has already failed. Ask the team to explain why. Surfaces risks that optimism hides. |
| 18 | [Cost-Benefit (Expected Value)](18%20Cost-Benefit%20(Expected%20Value).md) | Tally costs and benefits, weight by probability. Puts numbers on trade-offs. |
| 19 | [MoSCoW](19%20MoSCoW.md) | Sort requirements into Must have, Should have, Could have, Won't have. Forces clear commitment. |
| 20 | [Devil's Advocate (Red Team)](20%20Devil's%20Advocate%20(Red%20Team).md) | Institutionalized disagreement. Formally assign someone to attack the plan and find the flaws. |
| 21 | [Affinity Mapping](21%20Affinity%20Mapping.md) | Cluster a large pile of unstructured ideas into themes through silent grouping. |
| 22 | [Dot Voting](22%20Dot%20Voting.md) | Quick group prioritization. Fixed votes, scarce resources, honest signal about what people actually care about. |

## Structured Thinking Frameworks (23–27)

When the problem needs a specific thinking structure, not just more discussion.

| # | Method | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| 23 | [Six Thinking Hats](23%20Six%20Thinking%20Hats.md) | Force a group through six modes of thinking (facts, emotions, risks, benefits, creativity, process) one at a time. |
| 24 | [Walt Disney Method](24%20Walt%20Disney%20Method.md) | Separate three modes: Dreamer, Realist, Critic. Gives imagination, planning, and skepticism each their own space. |
| 25 | [Future Press Release](25%20Future%20Press%20Release.md) | Write the launch-day press release before building anything. If it reads as boring, the project is not worth doing. |
| 26 | [TRIZ](26%20TRIZ.md) | Systematic inventive problem-solving using 40 principles and contradiction analysis. For problems where two requirements conflict. |
| 27 | [Cynefin](27%20Cynefin.md) | Sense-making framework. Five domains (Clear, Complicated, Complex, Chaotic, Confused), each demanding a different response. |

## Process and Operations (28–31)

When you need a repeatable cycle for building, testing, or improving.

| # | Method | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| 28 | [Design Thinking](28%20Design%20Thinking.md) | Five-stage human-centered framework: Empathize, Define, Ideate, Prototype, Test. Iterative, not linear. |
| 29 | [Design Sprint](29%20Design%20Sprint.md) | Five-day compressed cycle: problem to tested prototype in one week. De-risk big bets before committing resources. |
| 30 | [Lean (PDCA)](30%20Lean%20(PDCA).md) | Plan, Do, Check, Act. The core improvement loop. Many small cycles beat one big plan. |
| 31 | [OODA Loop](31%20OODA%20Loop.md) | Observe, Orient, Decide, Act. Operational tempo framework. The faster loop wins. |
