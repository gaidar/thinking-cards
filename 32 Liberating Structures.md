# Liberating Structures

Liberating Structures is a catalog of 33 facilitation patterns by Henri Lipmanowicz and Keith McCandless. The standard meeting repertoire (presentations, open discussions, brainstorms) systematically excludes most voices. It may seem that open discussion gives everyone a fair chance. Yet, the loudest voices dominate, introverts self-censor, and the group settles on the first acceptable idea. These structures replace those defaults with tight, timed formats that include everyone.

**1. 1-2-4-All: the gateway structure.** Pose a question, then run four escalating rounds.
- **1 min:** individual reflection, silent writing.
- **2 min:** share with a partner, refine thinking.
- **4 min:** group of four, synthesize strongest ideas.
- **All:** surface top insights to the whole room.
- _"What is the single biggest blocker to our Q3 pipeline target?"_ 1-2-4-All yields 10x the input quality of an open floor discussion in under 15 minutes.

**2. TRIZ: surface counterproductive behavior.** Three questions in sequence:
- _"What would we do to guarantee the worst possible outcome for our partner onboarding?"_
- _"Of that list, what are we actually already doing?"_
- _"What will we stop doing, starting this week?"_
- Humor and inversion surface sacred cows that teams would never raise in direct conversation.

**3. Troika Consulting: solve individual problems.** Groups of three. Each person describes a challenge (4 min), then listens silently while the other two consult (4 min). Rotate.
- Silent listening forces actual reception of input instead of defending the current approach.
- _"Pick the one decision you are most stuck on this quarter. That is your Troika topic."_

**4. 15% Solutions: unstick big problems.** Ask: _"What is your 15%, the first small thing you can do right now, without anyone's permission?"_
- Short-circuits the excuse of needing approval, budget, or headcount before anything happens.
- _"You cannot hire two more SDRs this week. What can you do with what you have by Friday?"_
- Participants share and commit to acting within one to two weeks.

**5. What / So What / Now What? – structured debriefs.** Three-part reflection after any experience.
- **What?** Pure observation. What happened?
- **So what?** Interpretation. What does it mean?
- **Now what?** Action. What changes?
- _"The campaign generated 200 MQLs but only 8 converted to SQL. What happened, what does it mean, and what changes?"_

**6. Combine structures into longer arcs.** A 90-minute session might run 1-2-4-All for divergence, affinity mapping for clustering, dot voting for prioritization, 15% Solutions for commitment, then What/So What/Now What for reflection. Each structure runs 10 to 30 minutes. Full catalog at liberatingstructures.com.

**Practical notes:** Start with 1-2-4-All and one or two others. Use them consistently until natural, then expand. Teams accustomed to open discussion often resist tight time boxes and mandatory silent reflection. Hold the structure anyway. Yet, do not adopt all 33 at once. That reliably leads to abandoning the whole approach. 1-2-4-All alone, used in every team meeting for a month, will visibly change the dynamic.
