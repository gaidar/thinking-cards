# Crazy 8s

A fast sketching exercise from Google Ventures' Design Sprint. Fold a sheet into eight sections, then produce eight distinct ideas in eight minutes, one per section, one minute each. The time pressure shuts down your inner critic and forces you past the obvious first three ideas into genuinely different territory.

**1. Prepare the prompt.** You need a specific, visual challenge, something expressible as a quick sketch.

- _"Sketch the first screen a new user sees after signing up."_
- _"Design a one-screen dashboard for an MSP owner tracking 200 endpoints."_
- If the prompt cannot be sketched, reframe it until it can.

**2. Fold the paper.** Letter or A4, folded in half three times. Unfold for eight equal rectangles.

- Each person needs their own sheet and a thick marker. Pencils invite detail, which defeats the purpose. Markers force boldness.

**3. Eight ideas, eight minutes, one per box.** Start the clock. Sketch one distinct concept per minute.

- Stick figures and rough shapes are fine. Label each sketch with two or three words.
- Finish a box early? You are not pushing hard enough. Run dry around box five? That is exactly when the good ones start.

**4. Embrace the discomfort.** The first three sketches are variations on what you already had. The real value appears once you exhaust the obvious.

- Flip the core assumption, change the persona, invert the workflow, borrow from a different industry.

**5. Share and discuss.** Lay every sheet out. Each person walks through their eight sketches in 60 seconds. Others listen, then ask clarifying questions. No critique or ranking yet.

**6. Vote and develop.** Dot voting: three dots per person on the most promising sketches. Vote on anyone's work, including your own.

- Top-voted sketches become candidates for a detailed solution sketch or prototype. Voting is a signal, not a final decision.

**Practical notes:** Typically followed by a single-sketch exercise where each participant develops one idea into a three-panel storyboard. The method fails when people polish one sketch instead of moving to the next box. Enforce one-per-minute strictly. Works solo too: eight minutes with paper beats an hour staring at a screen.
